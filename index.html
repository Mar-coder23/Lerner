The Lerner Research Institute Computing Services (LRICS) group provides support for the desktop, server, and network-computing infrastructure of the Institute. LRICS is comprised of 21 information technology specialists providing server support, special project support and custom support of the desktop in biomedical and clinical research. The combined supported user base is greater than 2,000. Platforms supported include Windows, Macintosh and the Unix / Linux operating systems. The server environment, housed in the Cleveland Clinic Tier III data center, is monitored continuously by automated systems implemented at the enterprise level and specifically within the Lerner Research Institute. The Cleveland Clinic employs a defense in depth security model with multiple levels of advanced network- and host-based security.

The Lerner Research Institute provides access to five petabytes (usable) of network-attached storage. The data storage system is replicated from primary Dell-EMC Isilon grid storage nodes in the Tier III Cleveland Clinic data center in Brecksville, Ohio to secondary nodes in the Tier II Cleveland Clinic data center in Beachwood, Ohio. A large tape library provides additional backup services where required; offsite storage of cloned tapes is provide by contract services with Iron Mountain.

Various internal and external resources for data analysis can be provisioned to meet the specific needs of the investigators in the Lerner Research Institute. The Lerner Research Institute provides a high performance compute cluster (expansion in process to 1200 cores with immediate capacity to exceed 2000 cores). A 270 terabyte Intel Enterprise Edition Lustre scalable file system (expansion in process to >500 terabytes) is integrated into the clusterâ€™s InfiniBand backplane. Connectivity of the cluster to the Cleveland Clinic network backbone and directly to the replicated grid storage is provided by multiple 10 gigabit / sec uplinks. An extensive suite of analytic and development tools are available on the HPC and other processing resources, including an R environment and a dedicated 16 core SAS for Linux server. Investigators also have access to an Enterprise SAS Grid Engine / Teradata analytic system. Oracle, Microsoft SQL server and MySQL database capability is provided and maintained by two DBAs and a team of systems administrators. Compute services are also available from the Ohio Supercomputer Center.

Basic network service to desktop computers is 100 Mbps Ethernet, with 1 Gbps Ethernet available where needed. Backbone services within the Lerner Research Institute and between the Lerner Research Institute and the Tier III Cleveland Clinic data center operate at 10 Gbps. A standard Internet uplink is provided institutionally via redundant 10 Gbs links and a 10 Gbs connection to an Internet II point of presence is also available to investigators.

Emergency Support Requests

The following are considered an emergency:

  • Cannot log into a domain or clinic email account.
  • A computer, monitor or departmental printer that is unable to power on or fully boot.
  • A computer that can not print to a departmental printer or only installed printer.
  • Failure of pre-installed applications or operating system components critical to work,
    including Microsoft Office suite and Lawson.
  • Needed recovery of a deleted folder or file.
  • Out of storage space on a network share.
